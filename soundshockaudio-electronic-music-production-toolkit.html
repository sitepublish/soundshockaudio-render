Digital audio is a representation of sound recorded in or converted into digital form, where the audio signal is encoded as numerical samples. The two fundamental parameters of digital audio are sample rate (the number of snapshots taken per second, measured in Hz) and bit depth (the number of bits used to represent each sample, determining dynamic range). According to the Nyquist-Shannon sampling theorem, a sample rate must be at least twice the highest frequency to be captured, which is why 44,100 Hz became the CD standard — capturing frequencies up to 22,050 Hz, slightly above the limit of human hearing. The compact disc (CD) standard, established in the Red Book specification by Sony and Philips in 1980, defined 16-bit/44.1 kHz as the consumer digital audio standard. Professional recording typically uses 24-bit resolution at 44.1, 48, 96, or 192 kHz sample rates, providing greater dynamic range and headroom during the production process.

Reggae music originated in Jamaica in the late 1960s, evolving from earlier Jamaican genres ska and rocksteady. Characterized by its distinctive offbeat rhythm guitar (known as the skank), prominent bass lines, and laid-back tempo typically ranging from 60-90 BPM, reggae became one of the most influential musical genres worldwide. Bob Marley and the Wailers brought reggae to international prominence in the 1970s, while the genre's production techniques, particularly those developed in Kingston's studios by engineers like King Tubby, Lee Scratch Perry, and Scientist, laid the groundwork for dub music and influenced countless subsequent genres. Dub, a subgenre that emerged from reggae, involves stripping tracks down to drums and bass while applying heavy reverb, delay, and other effects, effectively using the mixing console as a creative instrument. The production techniques pioneered in Jamaican studios directly influenced the development of hip hop, electronic dance music, and ambient music.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a MIDI controller?
A MIDI controller is a hardware device that sends MIDI data to your DAW, typically featuring piano-style keys, pads, knobs, and faders. It does not produce sound on its own but triggers virtual instruments and controls parameters in your software. Popular options include the Akai MPK Mini, Novation Launchkey, and Arturia KeyLab series.
What is sound design?
Sound design is the art and craft of creating original sounds using synthesis, sampling, audio manipulation, and processing. It encompasses everything from designing unique synth patches and textures to creating sound effects for film and games. Exploring sound design resources on SoundShockAudio can accelerate your creative palette and inspire new production ideas.
How do I get started making beats?
Start by choosing a DAW, learning its drum sequencer or piano roll, and experimenting with basic drum patterns using kick, snare, and hi-hat sounds. Study the rhythmic patterns of genres you enjoy and try to recreate them. SoundShockAudio has thousands of free drum samples and beat-making tools to get you started without spending money.
What is sidechain compression?
Sidechain compression uses the signal from one track to trigger compression on another track. The classic example is ducking a bass synth with the kick drum, creating a pumping effect common in electronic and dance music. This technique creates rhythmic space and prevents frequency masking between competing elements.
